Understanding Financial Behavior

Finance isn’t just numbers — it’s human emotion, decision-making, and risk perception. Behavioral economics and psychology show us that people often make irrational financial choices.

Key Concepts

  • Loss Aversion: We fear losses more than we value gains.
  • Herd Behavior: We tend to follow the crowd, especially in markets.
  • Overconfidence: We often overestimate our financial knowledge.

Applications

Understanding these behaviors can help improve:

  • Investment strategies
  • Personal finance decisions
  • Policy-making and regulation
